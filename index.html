At Law and Justice Sector Secretariat...

(Office of the Secretriat, 7 th December 2021)

We work with Sector Agencies in PNG to create change through coordination, communication and collaboration because we understand different roles and responsiblities of the agencies that work trielessly to bring change and sustainability to create lasting change. We the link between different agencies and if we can link them together, we can work better and togther to achive our goals and vision, and we’re committed to improving it.

Our efforts connect people, and drive agencies to perform to standards and expectations, and empower people and donors to make more informed decisions—whether in addressing violence and petty crimes in the city, improve good goverance, fight against corruption or where to direct aid funding.

No matter the difference....

(Kali Dee, 7 th September 2021)

We put our law and justice sector agencies at the centre of our dicusssions and working processes. We use strategic managment and intervention tools and coordinate adhoc programs and projects and also to make sure programs and projects meet strategic KRA's and Vision.

We believe technology should be in the heart of our designs, plans and strategies that is to be more innovative. The law and justice agencies are requested to participate in the Annual Performance Report 2019-2020. This report is mandated by law as to reflect the performance of the law and justice sector, this report is intending to seek enrolement/Re-Enrolment into Post
endorsment and support for identifying their performace indicators.

LJS Woking Group members.

    ~ Candidates who are members of the law and justice working group and participate in monthly LJSWG meetings completed diploma programs at recognized institutions within the country or overseas.

    ~ Candidates who are senior representative of a agencies eg, Deputy Directors, Secretaries and Managers
      .

    ~ Candidates applying for specific program on offer related to their current employment and ;

    ~ Non-Nationals(South Pacific Islands and other Non-Nationals) may apply and admission would depend on avaliablity
      of space within the quota reserved for overseas students.

LJS AMT Members.

    ~ Candidates who are members of the law and justice sector activity managment team
      This members are either assigned members or officer holders in their certian departments eg,
      M&E manager, Policy and planning officers, ICT Members and deputies as well.

    ~ Grade 12 students from other Pacific Countries should complete the normal Application for Enrolment.
      Their admission into undergraduate programs will depend on the quota for Non-Nationals.
